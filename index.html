Jianan Zhang

 


I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Electronics at Peking University. I was a senior software engineer at Google during 2018-2023. I obtained B.E. degree from the Electronic Engineering Department at Tsinghua University in 2012, and M.S. and Ph.D. degrees from the Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems (LIDS) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 2014 and 2018, respectively.

My research interest is in network theory, with focus on resource allocations and robust network designs. My research has applications to cyber physical systems (such as smart transportation, smart grid and other interdependent networks), and data center networks. I am currently working on integrated sensing, communication and computation networks. My work builds on foundations in network optimization, stochastic control and graph theory.
